Case Study
Acme Bakery, Inc. v. LogistCo Refrigerated, LLC
$850,000 at stake
Acme contracted with LogistCo for weekly refrigerated delivery to five event venues. A truck failure during a July heatwave caused a 36-hour delay; the shipment spoiled. Acme lost three of its five venue contracts. Acme sues for consequential damages.
AI-Drafted Brief Paragraph

Acme's consequential damages were clearly within the parties' reasonable contemplation at the time of contract. The leading authority, Koufos v. Czarnikow Ltd. [1969] 1 AC 350 (The Heron II), establishes that damages are recoverable where the loss "was not unlikely" to result from breach. Victoria Laundry (Windsor) Ltd. v. Newman Industries Ltd. [1949] 2 KB 528 further held that a carrier with knowledge of the plaintiff's business is chargeable with foreseeing business losses. Most directly on point, Alpha Foods Distribution v. FreezeCorp Logistics, 421 F.Supp.3d 812 (S.D.N.Y. 2019), held that reputational damages flowing from a single breach were foreseeable where the defendant knew of the plaintiff's downstream client relationships.
