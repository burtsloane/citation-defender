What Happened
Early 2023
Attorney Researches Opposition Brief
Steven Schwartz (30 years experience) uses ChatGPT to find cases supporting Mata's opposition to a forum non conveniens motion.
ChatGPT Responds
Six Fabricated Citations Generated
Varghese v. China Southern, Shaboon v. Egyptair, Petersen v. Iran Air, Martinez v. Delta, Estate of Durden v. KLM, Miller v. United Airlines. All plausible. All fake. Complete with fabricated quotes and internal citations.
Verification Attempt
Schwartz Asks ChatGPT to Verify
ChatGPT confirms they are real. The attorney never checks Westlaw or Lexis.
March 1, 2023
Brief Filed in Federal Court
Six nonexistent cases submitted to a federal judge. Attorney of record Peter LoDuca signs without independent verification.
Spring 2023
Opposing Counsel Flags the Problem
Avianca's attorneys cannot locate any of the cited cases. They notify the court.
April 2023
Judge Orders Copies of Citations
Judge Castel demands the attorneys produce copies of the cited cases.
May 4, 2023
Attorney Admits ChatGPT Was the Source
Schwartz files an affidavit admitting he used ChatGPT and that all six cases were fabricated by the AI.
June 22, 2023
Sanctions Imposed
$5,000 fine. Required to notify real judges whose names appeared in fabricated opinions. Case described as "bogus judicial decisions with bogus quotes and bogus internal citations."
